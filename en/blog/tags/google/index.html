AI US Stock Market November Key Events Investment Research Report

Oct 31, 2025
Vibe Sparking

I. Overview of Core Events and Market Impact

Section titled “I. Overview of Core Events and Market Impact”

In November 2025, the US stock market faces a complex situation interwoven with five major categories of critical events, which will profoundly impact market direction. Based on current data analysis, the market is in a delicate balance of high valuations, low volatility, and high uncertainty.

  • The Supreme Court’s Tariff Ruling (November 5) is the biggest uncertainty factor this month. The Federal Court of Appeals has ruled that tariffs (reciprocal tariffs and fentanyl tariffs) implemented by Trump based on the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA) were illegal. The Supreme Court will hold oral arguments on November 5, with a final ruling expected in late 2025 or early 2026. This ruling involves a risk of nearly $200 billion in tariff refunds. If the Supreme Court overturns the tariffs, the market may see significant positive news; if it supports the government, it implies continued trade uncertainty.
  • Sino-US trade relations saw a brief détente at the end of October. After meeting in Korea, a one-year truce agreement was reached: the US will reduce tariffs on Chinese goods from 57% to 47% (fentanyl-related tariffs from 20% to 10%), and China will suspend rare earth export restrictions for one year. However, this is merely a tactical truce, not a structural solution. Both sides retain the option to restart the trade war, and the risk of escalation in November should be watched.
  • The government shutdown has lasted 29 days (as of October 31), making it one of the longest in US history. The Congressional Budget Office estimates the shutdown will result in $7-14 billion in permanent GDP loss. The suspension of key economic data releases creates a “data black hole,” forcing the Federal Reserve to formulate policy without core data on employment, inflation, etc. Approximately 750,000 federal employees have been furloughed, and SNAP (food stamp) benefits will cease on November 1, further impacting consumer demand.

Section titled “1. Palantir (PLTR) — Valuation Bubble Risk”

    • Extreme Valuation: Highest P/E in S&P 500. Q2 revenue first exceeded $1 billion, but market cap rationality requires years of high growth to materialize.
    • Dependence on Government Contracts: Although it has a multi-billion dollar long-term contract with the US Army, policy adjustments may compress orders.
    • Intensified Competition: Salesforce launched a national security business directly competing.
    • Analyst Caution: Divisions exist alongside valuation anxiety.
  • Opportunities:
    • Strong Commercial Segment (+93% YoY), robust AIP demand; deepening cooperation with Oracle and Snowflake; a billion-dollar contract with the British Armed Forces signals international expansion.

Section titled “2. AMD — Interplay of Expectations and Reality”

Tech Giants’ Q3 Earnings and Investment Playbook: AI‑Driven Opportunities and Risks

Oct 30, 2025
Vibe Sparking

In Q3 2025, U.S. tech giants delivered a mixed set of results. While the industry continues to benefit from the AI wave, performance diverged meaningfully. Google posted a historic milestone, surpassing $100B in quarterly revenue for the first time; Microsoft kept steady momentum with strong cloud and AI traction; Meta, meanwhile, was hit by a one‑time $16B deferred‑tax charge under a new U.S. tax law, triggering a 9% share‑price drop. In this market setup, investors need to capture opportunities precisely while balancing risk.

Q3 Earnings Snapshot: Divergence in the AI Era

Section titled “Q3 Earnings Snapshot: Divergence in the AI Era”

Google: A Historic Breakthrough with Across‑the‑Board Beats

Section titled “Google: A Historic Breakthrough with Across‑the‑Board Beats”

Alphabet set a new record in Q3, crossing the $100B mark for the first time with $102.3B in revenue, up 16% y/y and well above the $99.85B consensus. EPS reached $2.87, up 35% y/y.

The standout was Google Cloud: revenue hit $15.16B, up 34% y/y and above the $14.72B street view. Backlog surged to $155B, reflecting strong enterprise demand for AI infrastructure. Search and Other posted $56.57B, up 14.5% y/y, underscoring that the core search franchise remains healthy even amid competition from AI assistants like ChatGPT.

Shares rose more than 6% after hours, as the market grew more confident in Google’s AI transition and cloud‑business acceleration. Management raised full‑year capex guidance to $91–93B, signaling continued investment in AI infrastructure.

Meta: Strong Operations, Accounting Shock

Section titled “Meta: Strong Operations, Accounting Shock”

Meta reported Q3 revenue of $51.524B, up 26% y/y and above expectations. But a “tax storm” flipped sentiment.

Under the newly enacted U.S. tax bill (“One Big Beautiful Bill”), companies must recognize accumulated deferred‑tax assets in a one‑time reassessment. Because Meta holds substantial deferred‑tax assets and has heavy U.S.‑domiciled equity, it recognized a one‑off deferred‑tax expense of roughly $16B, driving an 83% y/y plunge in net income; EPS came in at $1.05, far below expectations.

Ex‑tax effects, Meta’s ad and Reels businesses remained strong. AI‑driven ad targeting and performance continued improving, with sales growth at 23% — outpacing Google’s 13% — highlighting Meta’s lead in AI‑powered ad tech.

Management guided 2025 capex at $70–72B, with another major step‑up in 2026. The aggressive investment plan shows Meta is spending heavily in the “race to AGI,” but it also raises near‑term margin and cash‑flow concerns.

Shares fell 9%, reflecting market pessimism around the one‑off tax expense and elevated forward spending.

Microsoft: Steady Growth with AI Dividends Materializing

Android Studio Finally Supports Gemini Agent for Automated Coding

Aug 3, 2025
Vibe Sparking

In the latest Android Studio Narwhal Feature Drop, Google has fully “embedded” Gemini into the IDE—not just for chatting, but acting like a “co-pilot” that can plan tasks and modify code in batches. You can use Rules to instantly lock in your project’s coding standards, and even connect your own Gemini API Key to unlock 1 million tokens of ultra-long context. In other words, Android development now has its first truly practical Agent Mode: it can read and write multiple files, run Gradle, create branches, submit PRs, and even write unit tests before fixing its own bugs—leaving you with nothing but nodding or shaking your head.

Introduction

Section titled “Introduction”

“How do you handle implementing a feature that requires changes across dozens of files?” In the past, we could only repeatedly switch windows, copy paths, and manually search through code. Now, Gemini Agent Mode takes over these mechanical steps—you just need one high-level instruction. The AI enhancements in Narwhal Feature Drop are designed to leave “thinking” to humans and “tedious work” to machines.

Get Started with Agent Mode in 1 Minute

Section titled “Get Started with Agent Mode in 1 Minute”
  1. Click Gemini → Agent in the sidebar, enter your goal: “Add dark mode to the homepage.”
  2. The Agent will first show a breakdown plan: which files need changes, which Gradle tasks to run.
  3. For each step, it generates code, applies patches, and runs tests—you can accept/reject each one.
  4. After everything passes, submit a PR with one click.

Why is it more powerful?

  • Supports cross-file batch modifications, breaking free from the “can only return one code snippet” bottleneck.
  • Built-in IDE permissions allow direct disk read/write and script execution.
  • Transparent planning with the ability to intervene and adjust at any time.

Custom Prompt Rules

Section titled “Custom Prompt Rules”

Want AI to always output Kotlin + Jetpack Compose? Open Prompt Library → Rules, write down your project conventions, and they’ll be automatically applied to every conversation thereafter. Teams can check .idea into version control, and rules will be shared along with the code—no more “verbal reminders” needed.

Connect Gemini 2.5 Pro, Unlock 1M Context

Section titled “Connect Gemini 2.5 Pro, Unlock 1M Context”

Free-tier models only have a few thousand tokens of context, often “forgetting” context during complex refactoring. Narwhal supports “Bring Your Own Key”: enter your Gemini API Key in settings to switch to 2.5 Pro, fitting entire code chains into a single conversation. Paid GCP projects enjoy this privilege by default.

Other AI-Powered Details

Section titled “Other AI-Powered Details”
  • XR Project Wizard: While primarily focused on XR, it also leverages Gemini logic prompts to automatically populate templates and best practices.
  • MCP Extensions: The initial version supports stdio transport and can further integrate with external tools like GitHub, with full streaming protocol support coming in the future.
  • Performance Optimizations: This stable release incorporates extensive feedback from Canary users, fixing issues like context loss and plan freezing.

Conclusion

Section titled “Conclusion”

Agent Mode isn’t just an “intelligent dialog box”—it’s a full-stack teammate that can modify code, run scripts, and write tests. It transforms Android Studio into a truly AI-powered IDE.
