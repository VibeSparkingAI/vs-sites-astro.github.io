Beyond Compute: An Investment Analysis of Promising AI Application Companies in the US Market for 2025

Section titled “Beyond Compute: An Investment Analysis of Promising AI Application Companies in the US Market for 2025”

As generative AI and agentic AI accelerate in adoption, the market’s focus is shifting from GPU vendors to platform companies that have already established moats in AI applications. This article provides an overview of key “Magnificent Seven of the AI Era” stocks in the US market, introducing their business fundamentals, technological advantages, and latest financial performance, while also looking ahead at their investment potential.

Disclaimer: This article is for informational purposes only and does not constitute specific investment advice. Investing involves risk; please make decisions with caution.

Palantir (PLTR): The Enterprise AI “Operating System”

Section titled “Palantir (PLTR): The Enterprise AI “Operating System””

Business Model & Performance Palantir is a renowned data analytics and intelligence platform that provides real-time decision support for government and enterprise clients. In the second quarter of 2025, the company’s revenue surpassed $1 billion, a year-over-year increase of approximately 48%. Its US commercial business nearly doubled year-over-year, and its government business grew by 53%. The company secured a 10-year, $100 billion contract with the U.S. Army (consolidating 75 programs into one) and received orders worth hundreds of millions from the U.S. Space Force and the “Maven Smart” system. This quarter’s outperformance led to an upward revision of its full-year revenue guidance to the ~$4.14 billion range. Furthermore, as of August 2025, Palantir’s stock has surged over 107% year-to-date, reflecting the market’s confidence in its growth prospects.

Technology & Moat Palantir’s platform deeply integrates algorithms with large-scale operations, logistics, and energy management, functioning like an enterprise-level “nervous system.” Its technology enables real-time integration of cross-departmental data (e.g., military dispatch, energy grid balancing), reducing manual delays. Key clients, such as defense agencies, often embed the Palantir platform into their core processes, making it difficult to replace once deployed and creating high switching costs. The company continues to invest in AI capabilities (like the Maven AI smart system), further solidifying its technological leadership.

Investment Potential Palantir is transitioning from a traditional project delivery model to a software subscription model, with high-growth revenue and a strong backlog of bookings supporting long-term growth. As enterprise and government clients increasingly rely on real-time intelligent decision systems, Palantir’s market opportunity is immense. Although the current stock price has risen significantly, its valuation remains challenging based on its expanding market cap; investors need to weigh its growth potential against the risks of a high valuation.

Cloudflare (NET): The “Enforcer” at the Internet’s Edge

Section titled “Cloudflare (NET): The “Enforcer” at the Internet’s Edge”

Business Model & Performance Cloudflare is a leading global network infrastructure platform, providing CDN, security, and edge computing services for websites and applications. In the second quarter of 2025, the company achieved revenue of $512.3 million, a 28% year-over-year increase. As of now, Cloudflare has approximately 266,000 paying customers, including 3,712 large customers (annual revenue >$100,000), indicating a highly sticky revenue stream. Notably, Cloudflare handles about 20% of the world’s internet traffic, meaning it controls a significant portion of the internet’s communication “choke points.”

Technology & Moat Cloudflare leverages its global network of nodes in over 300 cities to offer services like “Workers” edge computing and Zero Trust security, providing infrastructure for AI-driven applications. In the future, intelligent agents will need to access vast online resources, and as a central hub for network traffic, Cloudflare can become an essential part of their traffic path and security policy. The company is developing products like “Workers AI” to deploy AI inference capabilities to the edge for real-time computation, while also implementing a pay-per-crawl billing model to turn network traffic into a revenue-generating asset. Cloudflare doesn’t need to train its own models but controls the “interaction channel between models and the internet,” forming a unique moat.

Investment Potential Cloudflare’s performance has consistently accelerated in recent years, with its stock price gaining about 94% since the beginning of 2025. The expansion of its business scale (annualized revenue exceeding $2 billion) and a high net retention rate of 114% demonstrate strong customer demand. The company has a large market share in cloud security and content delivery and is continuously extending into AI infrastructure, showing clear growth expectations. Its current valuation is high, but given its position as a global traffic hub and its expansion momentum, it remains attractive for the long term.

CrowdStrike has evolved from traditional endpoint protection to a full-stack, cloud-native security provider. MongoDB (MDB): The “Memory Layer” for AI

Section titled “MongoDB (MDB): The “Memory Layer” for AI”

Management stated that many new customers are building infrastructure for AI applications, emphasizing MongoDB’s value in the AI era.

Technology & Moat MongoDB’s document model and flexible query capabilities differ from traditional relational databases (like Postgres), offering a natural advantage for AI applications that require processing massive amounts of unstructured or semi-structured data. MongoDB is continuously enhancing its platform’s capabilities in vector search and stream processing. For example, MongoDB recently launched a built-in vector search module to meet the demand for embedded retrieval. Its Atlas cloud service combines operational management with database processing, allowing enterprises to easily “keep” AI data in memory, supporting real-time updates and retrieval at the memory level. Once an enterprise adopts MongoDB for a critical application, replacement involves huge migration costs. Additionally, MongoDB’s ecosystem and compliance are maturing, and its open-source community is a driving force for technological innovation.

Investment Potential MongoDB is still in a loss-making expansion phase, prioritizing higher growth over current profitability. Analysts are generally bullish on its growth prospects—according to StockAnalysis, analysts covering it have a consensus “Strong Buy” rating, with a target price of around $300 per share.
