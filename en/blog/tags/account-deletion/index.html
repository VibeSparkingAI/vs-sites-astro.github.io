App Store Rejected Again: Understanding Account Deletion Requirements (Guideline 5.1.1)

Jan 5, 2026
Vibe Sparking

App Store Rejected Again: Understanding Account Deletion Requirements (Guideline 5.1.1)

Section titled “App Store Rejected Again: Understanding Account Deletion Requirements (Guideline 5.1.1)”

“If your app allows users to create accounts, it MUST also allow them to delete those accounts from within the app.” — Apple App Store Review Guideline 5.1.1(v)

As an iOS developer, there’s nothing more frustrating than having your app rejected by the App Store review team—especially when it’s for a requirement you didn’t even know existed. That’s exactly what happened to me with my app MoneyMind.

Today, I want to share my experience with Guideline 5.1.1(v) — the account deletion requirement — and help you avoid the same rejection.

The Rejection: Missing Account Deletion Process

Section titled “The Rejection: Missing Account Deletion Process”

After submitting an update to MoneyMind, I received this familiar email from Apple:

Guideline 5.1.1(v) - Account Deletion

We noticed that your app allows users to create accounts, but does not include a way for them to initiate deletion of their account from within the app.

Why This Happened

Section titled “Why This Happened”

MoneyMind allows users to:

  • Create accounts to track their finances
  • Sync data across devices
  • Store personal financial information

However, I had completely overlooked the requirement to provide a way for users to delete their accounts and associated data.

Understanding Guideline 5.1.1(v)

Section titled “Understanding Guideline 5.1.1(v)”

Apple’s account deletion requirement became effective on June 30, 2022. Here’s what you need to know:

The Core Requirement

Section titled “The Core Requirement”

If your app supports account creation, you MUST also let users:

  1. Initiate account deletion from within the app
  2. Delete the entire account record along with associated personal data
  3. Complete the process transparently

What Doesn’t Work

Section titled “What Doesn’t Work”
❌ Insufficient ApproachWhy It Fails
Only offering account deactivation/disablingUsers expect permanent deletion
Requiring users to contact customer supportCreates unnecessary friction
Directing users to website without in-app optionPoor user experience
No option at allDirect violation of guideline

What Apple Requires

Section titled “What Apple Requires”
✅ Required FeatureDescription
Easy to find optionTypically in app settings/account section
Complete deletionRemove account and associated personal data
Clear confirmationPrevent accidental deletions
Timely processingInform users if deletion takes time
Website link (if needed)Direct link to complete deletion on website

Implementing Account Deletion: Step by Step

Section titled “Implementing Account Deletion: Step by Step”

Step 1: Design the User Flow

Section titled “Step 1: Design the User Flow”

The account deletion option should be:

  1. Easy to find — Place it in Settings > Account section
  2. Clearly labeled — Use text like “Delete Account” or “Delete My Data”
  3. Multi-step confirmation — Prevent accidental deletions

Best Practices for Account Deletion

Section titled “Best Practices for Account Deletion”

Based on my experience and Apple’s guidelines, here are the key best practices:

1. Make It Discoverable

Section titled “1. Make It Discoverable”
  • ✅ Place in Settings > Account section
  • ✅ Use clear, prominent labeling
  • ✅ Consider adding it to a “Data & Privacy” section

2. Provide Clear Information

Section titled “2. Provide Clear Information”

Before users confirm deletion, tell them exactly what will be deleted:

  • Account information
  • Personal data
  • User-generated content
  • Purchase history (as applicable)

3. Appropriate Confirmation Steps

Section titled “3. Appropriate Confirmation Steps”
  • ✅ 2-3 confirmation steps to prevent accidents
  • ✅ Require re-authentication
  • ✅ Clear warning about irreversibility
  • ❌ Don’t make it frustratingly difficult

4. Handle Subscriptions Properly

Section titled “4. Handle Subscriptions Properly”
  • Warn users about active subscriptions
  • Provide link to manage/cancel subscriptions
  • Offer option to schedule deletion at subscription end
  • Use showManageSubscription (iOS 15+) or subscription management link

Common Questions Answered

Section titled “Common Questions Answered”

Can deletion be manual/async?

Section titled “Can deletion be manual/async?”

Yes! Apple allows manual deletion processes as long as you:

  • Inform users of the expected timeframe
  • Send confirmation when deletion is complete
  • Complete deletion within legally required timeframes

What if my app creates accounts automatically?

Section titled “What if my app creates accounts automatically?”

You still need to provide deletion options for these “guest” or auto-created accounts.

Do I need to delete user-generated content?

Section titled “Do I need to delete user-generated content?”

Yes, including posts, comments, reviews, photos, etc. Exceptions apply only if legally required to retain data.

Can I use customer service for deletion?

Section titled “Can I use customer service for deletion?”

Only if your app operates in a highly regulated industry (finance, healthcare, etc.). Otherwise, provide in-app deletion.

Pre-Submission Checklist

Section titled “Pre-Submission Checklist”

Before submitting your app to the App Store, verify:

  • App allows account creation
  • In-app account deletion option exists
  • Deletion option is easy to find
  • Clear warnings about data loss
  • Confirmation steps prevent accidents
  • Active subscriptions handled properly
  • Backend deletion process implemented
  • User informed of deletion timeframe (if not immediate)
  • Test deletion flow on real device

Conclusion

Section titled “Conclusion”

The account deletion requirement (Guideline 5.1.1(v)) is strictly enforced by Apple. Don’t make the same mistake I did—implement this feature before your first submission, not after a rejection.

Remember:

  1. Account creation = Account deletion capability
  2. Make it easy to find and use
  3. Clear warnings and confirmations
  4. Handle special cases (subscriptions, regulated industries)
  5. Test thoroughly before submission

By following these guidelines, you’ll avoid rejection and provide users with the data privacy controls they expect.

Good luck with your App Store submissions!
