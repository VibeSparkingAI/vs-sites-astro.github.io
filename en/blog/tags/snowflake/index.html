Beyond Compute: An Investment Analysis of Promising AI Application Companies in the US Market for 2025

Section titled “Beyond Compute: An Investment Analysis of Promising AI Application Companies in the US Market for 2025”

As generative AI and agentic AI accelerate in adoption, the market’s focus is shifting from GPU vendors to platform companies that have already established moats in AI applications. This article provides an overview of key “Magnificent Seven of the AI Era” stocks in the US market, introducing their business fundamentals, technological advantages, and latest financial performance, while also looking ahead at their investment potential.

Disclaimer: This article is for informational purposes only and does not constitute specific investment advice. Investing involves risk; please make decisions with caution.

Palantir (PLTR): The Enterprise AI “Operating System”

Section titled “Palantir (PLTR): The Enterprise AI “Operating System””

Business Model & Performance Palantir is a renowned data analytics and intelligence platform that provides real-time decision support for government and enterprise clients. In the second quarter of 2025, the company’s revenue surpassed $1 billion, a year-over-year increase of approximately 48%. Its US commercial business nearly doubled year-over-year, and its government business grew by 53%. The company secured a 10-year, $100 billion contract with the U.S. Army (consolidating 75 programs into one) and received orders worth hundreds of millions from the U.S. Space Force and the “Maven Smart” system. This quarter’s outperformance led to an upward revision of its full-year revenue guidance to the ~$4.14 billion range. Furthermore, as of August 2025, Palantir’s stock has surged over 107% year-to-date, reflecting the market’s confidence in its growth prospects.

Technology & Moat Palantir’s platform deeply integrates algorithms with large-scale operations, logistics, and energy management, functioning like an enterprise-level “nervous system.” Its technology enables real-time integration of cross-departmental data (e.g., military dispatch, energy grid balancing), reducing manual delays. Key clients, such as defense agencies, often embed the Palantir platform into their core processes, making it difficult to replace once deployed and creating high switching costs. The company continues to invest in AI capabilities (like the Maven AI smart system), further solidifying its technological leadership.

Investment Potential Palantir is transitioning from a traditional project delivery model to a software subscription model, with high-growth revenue and a strong backlog of bookings supporting long-term growth. As enterprise and government clients increasingly rely on real-time intelligent decision systems, Palantir’s market opportunity is immense. Although the current stock price has risen significantly, its valuation remains challenging based on its expanding market cap; investors need to weigh its growth potential against the risks of a high valuation.

Cloudflare (NET): The “Enforcer” at the Internet’s Edge

Section titled “Cloudflare (NET): The “Enforcer” at the Internet’s Edge”

Business Model & Performance Cloudflare is a leading global network infrastructure platform, providing CDN, security, and edge computing services for websites and applications. In the second quarter of 2025, the company achieved revenue of $512.3 million, a 28% year-over-year increase. As of now, Cloudflare has approximately 266,000 paying customers, including 3,712 large customers (annual revenue >$100,000), indicating a highly sticky revenue stream. Notably, Cloudflare handles about 20% of the world’s internet traffic, meaning it controls a significant portion of the internet’s communication “choke points.”

Technology & Moat Cloudflare leverages its global network of nodes in over 300 cities to offer services like “Workers” edge computing and Zero Trust security, providing infrastructure for AI-driven applications. In the future, intelligent agents will need to access vast online resources, and as a central hub for network traffic, Cloudflare can become an essential part of their traffic path and security policy. The company is developing products like “Workers AI” to deploy AI inference capabilities to the edge for real-time computation, while also implementing a pay-per-crawl billing model to turn network traffic into a revenue-generating asset. Cloudflare doesn’t need to train its own models but controls the “interaction channel between models and the internet,” forming a unique moat.

Investment Potential Cloudflare’s performance has consistently accelerated in recent years, with its stock price gaining about 94% since the beginning of 2025. The expansion of its business scale (annualized revenue exceeding $2 billion) and a high net retention rate of 114% demonstrate strong customer demand. The company has a large market share in cloud security and content delivery and is continuously extending into AI infrastructure, showing clear growth expectations. Its current valuation is high, but given its position as a global traffic hub and its expansion momentum, it remains attractive for the long term.

CrowdStrike (CRWD): The “Guardian” of Cloud Security

Section titled “CrowdStrike (CRWD): The “Guardian” of Cloud Security”

Business Model & Performance CrowdStrike is a leading global provider of a cloud-native cybersecurity platform. In the second quarter of fiscal year 2025, the company’s revenue reached $1.17 billion, a 21% year-over-year increase and a new record high; net new annual recurring revenue (ARR) was $221 million. By the end of the quarter, CrowdStrike’s total ARR was approximately $4.66 billion, up 20% year-over-year. The company’s financial report specifically highlighted the rapid growth of its “Falcon” security platform: over 1,000 customers have adopted the new licensing model based on Falcon Flex, and its cloud security business ARR has surpassed $700 million.

Technology & Moat CrowdStrike’s competitive advantage lies in its deep integration of AI with security protection, creating a “machine-speed” defense system. Its newly launched Charlotte AI, which acts as an automated analyst for enterprise security operations centers, saw its adoption rate increase by over 85% quarter-over-quarter. Each new protected device or deployed AI workload brings more security subscriptions (like Falcon Flex, identity security, etc.) and high renewal rates, creating a virtuous cycle. Meanwhile, the company has recently acquired big data pipeline companies like Onum to enhance its SIEM capabilities, further expanding its platform’s coverage. CrowdStrike has evolved from traditional endpoint protection to a full-stack, cloud-native security provider. Its mature enterprise user base and high retention rates have built a formidable barrier to entry.

Investment Potential CrowdStrike continues to lead in the security demand spurred by AI. Despite short-term stock price volatility, its long-term prospects are widely viewed as positive, considering its strong ARR growth, a net retention rate exceeding 4x, and innovative subscription models. With a current market cap of over $100 billion, investors need to monitor its future profitability and guidance. The company’s commitment to high R&D investment is expected to further solidify its position in the AI security market.

Snowflake (SNOW): The “Lubricant” for Data Cloud and AI

Section titled “Snowflake (SNOW): The “Lubricant” for Data Cloud and AI”

Business Model & Performance Snowflake operates a cross-cloud data platform that allows enterprises to unify, host, and analyze data from different cloud vendors. In the second quarter of fiscal year 2025, Snowflake achieved product revenue of $1.09 billion, a sharp 32% year-over-year increase. Its net retention rate was an impressive 125%, indicating that existing customers are continuously increasing their usage. As of the end of the reporting period, the company’s remaining performance obligations (RPO) reached $6.9 billion, up 33% year-over-year, signaling strong future performance. In the first two quarters of 2025, it added 533 new customers, a 21% increase, with 50 of them generating over $1 million in revenue. Notably, its deep collaboration with Microsoft is showing significant results: while AWS still accounts for the largest share of Snowflake’s overall revenue, the Azure cloud business has become its fastest-growing cloud platform, with revenue growth of 40%.

Technology & Moat Snowflake’s advantage lies in its cloud-native, multi-cloud compatible data storage engine and its innovative features tailored for AI scenarios. For example, its Cortex AI SQL allows users to call generative AI services directly with SQL, making data queries more intelligent. New products like Snowpipe Streaming and SnowConvert AI are also iterating rapidly. The platform’s consistency and high scalability meet the needs of enterprises building large-scale AI workflows. Once critical business operations depend on Snowflake, the switching costs are extremely high. As more organizations invest in AI and analytics projects, Snowflake’s position at the core of data flow makes it an indispensable infrastructure layer.

Investment Potential Snowflake is continuously investing in the AI ecosystem to enhance user stickiness. The company’s revenue and subscription numbers are growing rapidly, but it is still operating at a loss, which places high demands on its valuation. After its August 2025 earnings report, the company raised its full-year revenue guidance, and its stock price surged accordingly. For investors, key areas to watch are its path to profitability, improvements in net margin, and the long-term value growth brought by the implementation of AI applications.

MongoDB (MDB): The “Memory Layer” for AI

Section titled “MongoDB (MDB): The “Memory Layer” for AI”

Business Model & Performance MongoDB is a popular open-source document database, and its cloud service, Atlas, has become an enterprise-grade database alternative. In the second quarter of fiscal year 2025, the company’s revenue was $591.4 million, a 24% year-over-year increase. Atlas’s share of this continues to grow, with Atlas revenue increasing by 29% year-over-year, accounting for 74% of the quarter’s revenue. The company has a large customer base, with nearly 59,900 total customers as of July 31 (including nearly 2,800 added during the quarter), showing broad market penetration. Management stated that many new customers are building infrastructure for AI applications, emphasizing MongoDB’s value in the AI era.

Technology & Moat MongoDB’s document model and flexible query capabilities differ from traditional relational databases (like Postgres), offering a natural advantage for AI applications that require processing massive amounts of unstructured or semi-structured data. MongoDB is continuously enhancing its platform’s capabilities in vector search and stream processing. For example, MongoDB recently launched a built-in vector search module to meet the demand for embedded retrieval. Its Atlas cloud service combines operational management with database processing, allowing enterprises to easily “keep” AI data in memory, supporting real-time updates and retrieval at the memory level. Once an enterprise adopts MongoDB for a critical application, replacement involves huge migration costs. Additionally, MongoDB’s ecosystem and compliance are maturing, and its open-source community is a driving force for technological innovation.

Investment Potential MongoDB is still in a loss-making expansion phase, prioritizing higher growth over current profitability. The company continues to invest heavily in marketing and R&D and is optimistic about its future AI business. Analysts are generally bullish on its growth prospects—according to StockAnalysis, analysts covering it have a consensus “Strong Buy” rating, with a target price of around $300 per share. As a typical high-growth tech stock, MongoDB has high volatility, and investors should manage risk accordingly.

Tesla (TSLA): The Pioneer of Digitalized and Embodied AI

Section titled “Tesla (TSLA): The Pioneer of Digitalized and Embodied AI”

Business Model & Performance Tesla is not only the world’s largest electric vehicle manufacturer but also a rapidly emerging AI and energy company. In the second quarter of 2025, the company’s revenue was $22.5 billion (a 12% year-over-year decline), with a net income attributable to common stockholders of $1.2 billion. This quarter, the company announced the launch of its robotaxi service in Austin and delivered its first fully autonomous vehicle. Additionally, the company deployed its first batch of Megapack energy storage units in Shanghai, continuing to set new records in energy storage deployment. Tesla also revealed that its autonomous driving development is based entirely on in-car cameras (no radar) and that it continuously collects road data from millions of online vehicles to optimize its models through neural networks.

Technology & Moat Tesla’s uniqueness lies in its true end-to-end data loop: it trains AI models with real-world data from hundreds of millions of electric vehicles, creating a perceptual capability that is unmatched. The massive deployment of Megapacks on the residential and grid edge can coordinate with the smart grid to achieve intelligent energy management. Even when facing challenges from traditional auto and tech giants, Tesla’s vast amount of real-world data and its fully autonomous R&D and manufacturing systems give its self-driving and robotics projects (like the Optimus robot) a difficult-to-replicate advantage. In fact, Tesla’s current market cap has surpassed $1 trillion, far exceeding the combined value of traditional automakers, reflecting the market’s expectations for its future AI + energy vision.

Investment Potential Tesla is valued within a “company of the future” framework. Its short-term business is heavily influenced by vehicle model updates and the macroeconomic environment, but its long-term returns depend on the maturity of its autonomous driving, robotics, and energy businesses. Once its Robotaxi and FSD businesses scale, they will open up a new market worth hundreds of billions of dollars. Investors need to monitor Tesla’s technological progress and the regulatory environment, but due to its unique digital ecosystem, Tesla remains an indispensable part of any AI investment portfolio.

Axon Enterprise (AXON): The AI Control Center for Public Safety

Section titled “Axon Enterprise (AXON): The AI Control Center for Public Safety”

Business Model & Performance Axon provides an integrated solution of hardware (like tasers, body cameras) and software (Evidence.com evidence management platform, real-time dispatch, AI-assisted tools) for law enforcement and public safety departments. In the second quarter of 2025, the company’s revenue was $669 million, a 33% year-over-year increase. Software and services revenue was $2.92 billion (+39%), while device sales revenue was $3.76 billion (+29%). By the end of the reporting period, the company’s annual recurring revenue (ARR) was approximately $1.2 billion, up 39% year-over-year, with a net retention rate of 124%. The company continues to differentiate its products by introducing AI features (like smart tagging for evidence retrieval, real-time translation, etc.).

Technology & Moat Axon’s ecosystem has become the de facto standard for US law enforcement agencies. At the legislative level, many states have mandated the use of data from body-worn cameras as a procedural norm (e.g., laws requiring video evidence), making the Evidence.com platform an “enterprise operating system” with extremely high switching costs. Once an agency builds its operations on the Axon platform, switching to another provider would require not only revising regulations but also extensive personnel training. At the same time, Axon creates synergy by continuously launching new devices (TASER 10, tasers, in-car cameras, etc.) and software features (cloud-based real-time monitoring, training simulations, drone systems, etc.), tightly bundling hardware use with software subscriptions. In terms of AI applications, the company prides itself on being the first in the industry to launch AI-driven policing tools and can further deepen its moat by using machine vision and big data analytics to improve policing efficiency.

Investment Potential Axon’s performance has shown continuous accelerated growth, and its 2025 revenue guidance has been raised. Its broad customer base (involving thousands of law enforcement agencies) and highly sticky subscription business give its performance high visibility. As of August 29, Axon’s market cap was approximately $58.7 billion, placing it in a leading position in the public safety sector. Although the security sector typically has more conservative valuations, Axon combines the triple advantages of hardware, advanced software, and AI, giving it outstanding long-term growth potential.

Summary

Section titled “Summary”

The companies listed above represent the core platforms of the second phase of AI development. They have each formed deep moats in data processing, network infrastructure, security, and core applications. As AI agents gradually enter the physical world (from energy and power grids to city management and public safety), the reliance on these platforms will only increase. In terms of investment, while these stocks carry risks related to valuation or operational volatility, their fundamentals show significant growth momentum and strong moats. A reminder to investors: technological innovation is rapid, so it is essential to dynamically track performance and the competitive landscape and to allocate assets rationally.
