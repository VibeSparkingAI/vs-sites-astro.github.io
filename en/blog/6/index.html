KestrelAI: Long-term Research Assistants with Self-Scheduling

Section titled “KestrelAI: Long-term Research Assistants with Self-Scheduling”

In the world of AI research assistants, most tools focus on quick, one-shot responses. KestrelAI takes a different approach—it’s a local-first research assistant that self-schedules and executes support tasks over long horizons, allowing you to stay focused on complex, novel work while it handles mechanical tasks in the background.

What is KestrelAI?

Section titled “What is KestrelAI?”

Kestrel is a local-first research assistant designed for long-running research tasks. What makes it unique:

  • Self-Scheduling: Automatically plans and schedules work, tracks progress, and pivots when stalled
  • Long-Horizon Execution: Runs for hours or days, providing auditable checkpoints for review
  • Local-First: Built to run on small LLMs (1-7B) via Ollama—laptop/desktop friendly
  • Source Provenance: Maintains clear records of consulted searches and their impact on findings

GitHub Repository: dankeg/KestrelAI Author: Ganesh Danke License: MIT

The Problem Kestrel Solves

Section titled “The Problem Kestrel Solves”

When doing deep research work, you often encounter time-consuming mechanical tasks:

  • Comparing tools and frameworks
  • Gathering real-world usage examples
  • Extracting performance claims from documentation
  • Assembling comprehensive comparisons

These tasks interrupt your flow on the actual novel work. Kestrel handles this background research, surfacing organized evidence when you need it.

Example Use Case

Section titled “Example Use Case”

Imagine you’re interpreting a frontier research paper and planning a novel study. You need to decide between Polars vs. pandas vs. Spark for your data processing.

Instead of spending hours manually researching, you tell Kestrel:

“Compare Polars, pandas, and Spark for data processing. Gather real-world usage, performance benchmarks, and use case recommendations.”

Kestrel then runs in the background:

  1. Searches for documentation and benchmarks
  2. Filters relevant sources
  3. Extracts performance claims
  4. Assembles a reviewable comparison

Hours later, you have an organized report ready for analysis—not scattered across browser tabs.

Architecture

Section titled “Architecture”

Kestrel uses a two-agent architecture:

Research Agent

Section titled “Research Agent”

Executes concrete micro-tasks:

  • Search → Retrieve → Filter → Extract → Narrow summary
  • Proposes specific follow-ups when evidence warrants it

Orchestrator

Section titled “Orchestrator”

Plans and coordinates the overall effort:

  • Schedules work across agents
  • Tracks progress and blockers
  • Pivots when stalled
  • Emits checkpoints you can accept or adjust

HummingbirdQuick breadth-first sweeps; targeted lookupsMinutes–~2h

Breakthrough Method for Agile AI-Driven Development — A framework that enables AI to participate in development as an agile team.
